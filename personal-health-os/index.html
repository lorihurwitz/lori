Competitive Landscape

The front door to care is already changing

People don't wait for a doctor's appointment to answer a health question anymore. They ask ChatGPT, search symptoms, message a friend in medicine. Roughly 1 in 3 adults now use AI for health advice before seeing a doctor, and 3 in 5 have used AI tools for health questions in the past three months. The question is not whether AI becomes the default first stop in care. It already is.

The more interesting question is which platforms earn the right to move from information to actual guidance — and do it in a way that democratizes access to care that has historically been gated by physician scarcity, cost, and geography. If you grew up with a doctor in your family, you had an unfair advantage: real-time, trusted, personalized guidance whenever something felt off. Everyone else waited 40 days for an appointment or Googled their symptoms. AI can collapse that gap permanently — but only if the platform on the other end has earned the right to be trusted with medical decisions.

"Growing up with a physician parent and being a physician myself, I know the magical feeling of having instant trusted advice anytime you need it about your health. How do we democratize that magical feeling to everyone?"

— Dr. Muthu Alagappan, CEO & Founder, Counsel Health

The incumbents are structurally misaligned with this moment. Below is a full accounting of who is competing for the front door, why each has a ceiling, and which three companies are building the right answer.

The Existing Landscape — Why Each Has a Structural Ceiling
ChatGPT / OpenAI Health Watch
ChatGPT Health launched Jan 2026 · 40M+ daily users ask health questions
The most direct evidence that the behavior has already shifted. People are reaching for the most capable AI they have access to when something worries them — not because OpenAI built a health product, but because it's already there. No physician oversight; safety guardrails are self-imposed by the model.
Ceiling: The liability exposure of a wrong medical response at scale is entirely unresolved. One incident creates regulatory risk for the entire category — and a clear opening for supervised alternatives.
Amazon One Medical Incumbent
$3.9B acquisition (2023) · Health AI launched Jan 2026 · 150M+ Prime members
Acquired One Medical for brick-and-mortar primary care and telehealth, then launched Health AI with EHR integration and clinical guardrails. Massive distribution advantage. Deep pockets to iterate.
Ceiling: Explicitly designed to complement providers, not replace the front door. Still episodic and transactional at its core. Not structurally incentivized to build physician supervision at scale — the liability and infrastructure requirements break their model.
Google Health
Med-PaLM 2 · Search health features · Health system pilots
Search is still the #1 source of consumer health information globally — 8.5B+ daily queries. Med-PaLM 2 near-expert on medical licensing exams. Top-of-funnel dominance is structural.
Ceiling: No clinical accountability, no continuous relationship, no monetization model that requires a physician in the loop. Distribution without trust architecture.
Teladoc Health Incumbent
NYSE: TDOC · ~$700M revenue · 80M+ virtual visits · $13.7B Livongo writedown
Largest telehealth network in the US. Classic model: on-demand video visit, scheduled, transactional. A better phone call — not a continuous health relationship.
Ceiling: Episodic by design. Re-rated down as the market recognized that episodic telehealth doesn't create durable engagement or switching costs. Cannot structurally become what continuous care requires.
K Health Closest Analog
~$370M raised · Cedars-Sinai, Mayo Clinic partnerships
AI-first primary care using clinical data from millions of patients to power differential diagnosis. One of the closest structural analogs — but focused on AI-first diagnosis, not physician-supervised continuous guidance.
Key difference: K Health's AI surfaces diagnosis suggestions. Counsel's physician layer resolves 96% of issues in real time. Fundamentally different clinical accountability model.
Nurse Lines / Managed Care Incumbent
Embedded in employer and payer benefits · Legacy model
Traditional 24/7 nurse triage embedded in enterprise benefits stacks. Protocols drive over-escalation by design — liability-minimizing, not outcome-optimizing. Resolution rates of ~40–50%.
This is the line item Counsel replaces. Saves $381/member/year vs. the current model. The ROI argument is the enterprise sales pitch.
Three Expressions of the Same Shift

These companies are not competing with each other. They represent three different entry points into the same structural thesis: AI democratizing access to care that was previously gated by physician scarcity, cost, or geography — across acute care, mental health, and proactive health optimization.

Expression 01 — Acute & Continuous Care
Counsel Health
$36M raised a16z · GV · Pear VC Series A Licensed all 50 states Founded 2023
Target Investment

Counsel is the most direct expression of the democratization thesis: physician-supervised AI that makes real medical guidance available to anyone, instantly, without a 40-day wait. Every interaction is triaged by medical AI; licensed physicians step in for diagnosis, prescriptions, referrals, and follow-up until the issue is resolved — all asynchronously, through messaging, at a price point that works through payers and employers rather than requiring individuals to pay concierge rates.

The model is asynchronous by design, not by constraint. Inbox messaging rates in healthcare have nearly doubled annually for several consecutive years — people already prefer to communicate about health through text rather than scheduling a call. Counsel is building the clinical infrastructure for that modality to be taken seriously as a care model, not treated as a bolt-on feature. Their physician platform makes it economically viable to deliver supervised care at scale without the unit economics breaking.

On the enterprise side, Counsel replaces nurse lines as a direct line item in payer and employer benefits stacks. The replacement logic is simple: 96% issue resolution versus ~40–50% for nurse lines, physician response in 2 minutes versus 38-day appointment wait, and $381 saved per engaged member per year. The ROI argument is the close.

Why this wins

Every other platform in this space has made a deliberate choice to keep physicians out of the loop wherever possible — the cost economics are obvious. Counsel made the opposite bet: physician oversight is the product, not a cost to be engineered away. That architectural decision creates a moat that pure-AI competitors cannot cross without rebuilding from scratch, and that Amazon, Google, and OpenAI are structurally incentivized not to build. The regulatory environment is accelerating in Counsel's direction: CMS and FDA's December 2025 ACCESS and TEMPO programs created explicit incentives for clinician-in-the-loop AI, and multiple commercial payers followed in February 2026.

Expression 02 — Mental Health & Continuous Support
Jimini Health
$25M raised M13 · Town Hall · LionBird · Zetta Seed $17M raise March 2026 Founded 2023
Comparable

Mental health has the same access problem as primary care — compounded by stigma and an even more acute provider shortage. Jimini applies the same clinician-supervised AI architecture to behavioral health: an AI therapist assistant (Sage) handles between-session engagement while licensed therapists supervise every interaction. The gap between sessions is where most of daily mental health experience actually happens, and the traditional model leaves it entirely unsupported.

The go-to-market is B2B2C into behavioral health provider organizations rather than direct to consumers — a different distribution path than Counsel, but the same underlying thesis: AI extends the reach of scarce clinicians rather than replacing them, and trust comes from keeping a clinician in the loop. The $17M seed raise in March 2026 brings the total to $25M, backed by a strong syndicate including M13 and Town Hall Ventures. The CEO previously co-founded Immunai, which reached a $1B+ valuation.

Why this matters for the thesis

Jimini validates that clinician-supervised AI is a category-level architecture, not a bet specific to Counsel. Two companies building the same structural answer in adjacent verticals — acute care and mental health — is a signal. The CMS/FDA ACCESS and TEMPO programs in December 2025 created regulatory tailwinds for both simultaneously, which reinforces that the architecture is right, not just the individual companies.

Expression 03 — Proactive & Preventive Health
Superpower Health
$34M raised Forerunner Ventures Series A 150k-person waitlist (Apr 2025) Founded 2023
Adjacent

Superpower is the proactive expression of the thesis — democratizing access to the kind of comprehensive, data-driven health optimization that has historically been reserved for people wealthy enough to afford concierge medicine. Comprehensive biomarker panels, personalized longevity protocols, an AI health coach that compounds over time: these have always existed, but only at price points that excluded most people. Superpower brings the concierge model to a consumer price point.

It is not competing with Counsel for the same user moment. Counsel is where you go when something feels wrong. Superpower is where you go to stay ahead of something feeling wrong. Together they represent the full arc of continuous care — reactive and proactive, acute and preventive. Forerunner's backing is the right signal: this is a consumer identity and behavior-change play as much as a healthcare one, and Forerunner has a track record of identifying those shifts early.

Why this matters for the thesis

The 150k-person waitlist before a full consumer launch is a demand signal that consumers want proactive, data-rich health intelligence at a new price point. The behavioral shift toward proactive care is the longest-duration part of the thesis — harder to prove, but potentially the largest value pool. Superpower is the earliest and clearest bet on that leg of the shift.
